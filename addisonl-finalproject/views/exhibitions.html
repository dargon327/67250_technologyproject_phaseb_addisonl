Current Exhibition: 20th Century Art

March 13 - June 12, 2026

MonoMuse cycles through different time periods, showcasing the most influential artists and movements that shaped the art world. This cycle turns its focus to the 20th century, an era of radical experimentation, bold expression, and groundbreaking ideas. Featuring works by two iconic artists, this exhibition invites visitors to explore how art challenged convention and redefined what it means to see, feel, and create.

Francis Bacon

Francis Bacon (1909-1992) was an Irish-born British figurative painter known for his raw, emotionally charged imagery. His work often depicted the human body in states of distortion and anguish, drawing on themes of isolation, mortality, and the human condition.

Bacon's style is rooted in expressionism and figurative art, characterized by bold brushwork, smeared forms, and claustrophobic compositions. His iconic "screaming pope" series, inspired by Velázquez's portrait of Pope Innocent X, remains among the most recognized works of 20th-century art.

At MonoMuse, visitors will experience a curated selection of Bacon's paintings and studies, highlighting his intense use of color, texture, and psychological tension.

Andy Warhol

Andy Warhol (1928-1987) was an American artist, filmmaker, and leading figure of the Pop Art movement. Born and raised in Pittsburgh, Warhol transformed everyday consumer objects into fine art, blurring the line between commercial and high culture.

Warhol's style embraced mass production techniques such as silkscreen printing, repetition, and vivid color palettes. His work challenged traditional ideas of originality and authorship, making him one of the most influential and provocative artists of the 20th century.

This exhibition goes beyond the familiar icons to explore Warhol's lesser-known experiments in film, sculpture, and collaborative art, celebrating Pittsburgh's own contribution to the global art world.
