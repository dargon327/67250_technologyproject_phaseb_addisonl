About MonoMuse

MonoMuse is a contemporary art museum in the heart of Pittsburgh, dedicated to showcasing the full spectrum of creative expression. We bring together emerging and established artists working across painting, sculpture, photography, digital media, and immersive installations.

Our Mission

To make art accessible, engaging, and meaningful for all. MonoMuse is committed to creating experiences that inspire curiosity, spark dialogue, and strengthen the bond between art and community.

Our Vision

We envision a world where every person - regardless of background or experience - can find connection, meaning, and joy through art. MonoMuse strives to be a space where diverse perspectives are celebrated and creative expression is a shared language.

Collection Highlights

Our rotating exhibitions feature a wide range of works, including large-scale immersive installations, mixed-media sculptures, contemporary photography, and interactive digital pieces. Recent highlights include a light-based installation series by six international artists and a groundbreaking exhibit exploring sound as physical form.

Current Exhibitions

Francis Bacon: Figures in Motion

This exhibition presents a rare collection of Francis Bacon's most striking figurative works, exploring themes of isolation, raw emotion, and the distortion of the human form. Visitors will experience Bacon's intense use of color and brushwork up close.

Andy Warhol: Beyond the Surface

Celebrating Pittsburgh's own Andy Warhol, this exhibition goes beyond the iconic Campbell's Soup Cans to explore Warhol's lesser-known experiments in film, sculpture, and collaborative art. A must-see for anyone interested in the intersection of art and popular culture.

Our History

MonoMuse was founded in 2018 by a group of Pittsburgh artists and educators who believed their city deserved a museum that reflected the energy and diversity of contemporary art. What began as a series of pop-up exhibitions in repurposed warehouses grew into a permanent institution within two years.

The museum opened its doors at its current location on Morewood Avenue in 2020, occupying a renovated mid-century building that blends industrial architecture with modern gallery design.

Major Milestones

  • 2018 - MonoMuse founded; first pop-up exhibition draws 800 visitors
  • 2019 - Partnership with Carnegie Mellon University for student artist residency program
  • 2020 - Permanent location opens on Morewood Avenue
  • 2022 - Annual attendance surpasses 50,000 visitors
  • 2024 - Awarded the Pittsburgh Cultural Trust Award for Innovation in the Arts
  • 2025 - Launch of the MonoMuse Digital Gallery, bringing exhibitions online

Take the 61A, 61B, 61C, or 71B to Forbes and Morewood. The museum is a one-minute walk from the stop.

From I-376, take the Oakland/Forbes Avenue exit. Head east on Forbes Avenue, then turn left onto Morewood Avenue. The museum is on your right.

Street parking is available along Morewood Avenue. The nearby Morewood Parking Garage offers hourly and daily rates. Free parking is available on weekends.
